FPV Drone Building & Assembly Certification Exam
Soldering • Wiring • Frame Selection • Stack Assembly • Power Systems
Difficulty
Easy → 1 Point
Medium → 2 Points
Hard → 3 Points

Goal: These questions are intentionally designed to make students think like real FPV builders rather than memorize definitions.

SECTION 1: SOLDERING FUNDAMENTALS
Q1

A solder joint appears shiny but breaks when the wire is lightly moved. What is the most likely cause?

A. Excessive flux usage
B. Cold solder joint hidden under surface solder
C. Excessive copper thickness
D. High quality solder

Answer: B

Q2

Which is generally the most reliable soldering sequence?

A. Heat solder → Heat pad → Add wire
B. Heat wire only
C. Heat pad and wire together, then apply solder
D. Melt solder first and drop it onto the pad

Answer: C

Q3

A student increases iron temperature from 350°C to 480°C to solder faster. What is the biggest risk?

A. Better conductivity
B. Pad delamination and PCB damage
C. Reduced oxidation
D. Stronger solder joints

Answer: B

Q4

Why is flux often more important than increasing soldering temperature?

A. It reduces wire resistance
B. It removes oxidation and improves wetting
C. It increases current carrying capacity
D. It cools the solder

Answer: B

Q5

A solder joint forms a perfect sphere sitting on the pad. What does this usually indicate?

A. Excellent wetting
B. Excessive heat transfer
C. Poor wetting or contamination
D. High current capability

Answer: C

Q6

What is the primary reason builders pre-tin wires?

A. Improve appearance
B. Reduce solder consumption
C. Improve final bond quality and heat transfer
D. Reduce wire flexibility

Answer: C

Q7

Which soldering mistake most commonly damages FC pads?

A. Using flux
B. Repeated reheating and pulling wires
C. Using silicone wires
D. Cleaning with IPA

Answer: B

Q8

A solder joint looks dull gray instead of shiny. What should be suspected first?

A. Strong joint
B. Cold joint
C. Excessive copper
D. Excessive current capacity

Answer: B

Q9

What is the safest way to remove excess solder from a pad?

A. Knife
B. Wire cutter
C. Solder wick
D. Screwdriver

Answer: C

Q10

When soldering XT60 connectors, the greatest danger is:

A. High resistance
B. Connector melting from prolonged heating
C. Too much flux
D. Wire flexibility

Answer: B
